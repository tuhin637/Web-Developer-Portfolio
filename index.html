This thesis investigates the comparative performance of Random Forest, XGBoost, and Deep Learning models for early detection of heart disease. Using the UCI Heart Disease dataset, the study applies feature engineering, cross-validation, and hyperparameter tuning to achieve high-accuracy predictions, with the goal of building a clinically deployable decision-support tool via Streamlit.
